About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science with DATA61 | CSIRO and the School of Information Technology, Monash University, Melbourne.

My academic background is in biology, researching the collective decision making of social insects. Switching to the Faculty of Information Technology for my PhD allows me to explore mathematical methods for the description and modeling of collective behaviour dynamics.

In my PhD project, I investigate the self-organised infrastructure construction in leafcutter ants. For more information on this and previous projects, visit the Research section.

Research Interests

Social insect societies have a fundamentally different form of organisation from that of humans. Without centralised control by a competent individual, self-organising social insects still perform feats of remarkable complexity. How do the - relatively simple - individuals achieve this? How can we describe their behaviour mathematically?

When not working on self-organisation in ants, I take an interest in swarm robot control and social network analysis.
