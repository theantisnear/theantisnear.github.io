I am a computational biologist interested in complex biological systems. I recently completed my PhD with Prof. Bernd Meyer and A/Prof. Martin Burd at the Monash University Faculty of Information Technology and CSIRO|Data61. In my PhD project I combined biological experiments, automated data extraction and analysis, and modelling of behavioural and evolutionary dynamics.

The focus of my research is the collective construction behaviour in ants. Their colonies build complex nests and cleared highways, reminiscent of human cities and road networks. The larger question I am adressing is how groups of individuals with very limited information about their environment perform such complex tasks.

For more information on my work, visit the Research section.

When not working on social insects I take an interest in the extrapolation of these concepts to swarm robot control and human social dynamics.
